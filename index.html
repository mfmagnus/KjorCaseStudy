Statoil Fuel & Retail

Putting the passenger in the drivers seat.

A story of how Statoil and MediaFront made long journeys more entertaining by providing refreshments through gameplay along the way.

About the client

Statoil Fuel and Retail needed to increase traffic to the stores.  Statoil F&R see a huge potential if they are able to create a strong customer relationship program that increases preference for Statoil among competing gas stations.

Insight

The petrol industry is homogenous. There are limited of no differentiation in the category. Preference in the category is randomized and heavily dependent on location and where you are when you feel the need to refuel your car. The product are basically the same, there is limited variation in services between stations and the category if overladed with  boring tactical offers selling sausages and pastries. This need to be challenged. 

The challenge

The drivers preferences and coincidences decide where customers stop to refuel their car. Could we shift the discussion to passengers?  Could the car trip be more fun?And even appear more innovative. Today's entertainment booklets for kids are perceived as outdated, costly in production and nobody picks them up.

Technology

How do we use exciting new technology to give an immersive and believable feeling of being a "real" backseat driver, while creating addictive and rewarding gameplay.

Speed & Position

Smartphones and tables these days have access to a GPS which becomes the basis for achieving a two key functions; Allowing the car in the game to reflect the speed of the vehicle in which the player actually sits, and knowing exactly where the player is relative to actual Statoil stations.

Speed

The first technical challenge lies in using the GPS data in creating an immersive and responsive gameplay that truly gives the passenger the feel of being in control of the car. With intelligent code that balances accuracy with battery usage, we were able to create a realistic gameplay feeling that doesn't end too soon.

Position

The GPS also allows us to pinpoint quite exactly where the player is at all times. By also having the coordinates to all Statoil stations across the country, concepts such as geo-fencing allows accurate detection of the player being within medium or close range of any given station. Combined with knowing the speed, we have a way to also detect players actually stopping at a Statoil station.

Controls

With accelerometers and gyroscopes now standard in phones and tables, these become the basis for the player's interaction in the game; Steering becomes a natural tilting from side to side much like using a steering wheel, and mini games to refill the tank provides a more all-axis challenge to keep the nozzle from spilling.

The Third Dimension

In line with the concept of creating a realistic game world, the choice fell to using Unity3D which is a world renowned game development system, a powerful rendering engine fully integrated with a complete set of intuitive tools and rapid workflows to create interactive 3D content.

Spawners & The Open Road

The idea and concept of spawners is simple; It can be viewed as logic that allows for spawning (or creating) and destroying (or removing) game objects such as coins, road tiles, terrain and obstacles in an intelligent manner. One challenge was to giving the player a potentially endless journey, another to create a balanced and entertaining game.



Curves & Collisions

Enter curves and collision detection! By using smart and well tuned curves, we can control such concepts like difficulties by balancing the spawning of coins vs. obstacles. Collisions are detected between the player and game objects allowing for collecting rewards and avoiding crashing into obstacles. The road shoulder can be used as a safe haven from obstacles, but it too detects collisions with the player and will slowly drain coins.
